About This Course

This combined lecture and lab course explores the relationship between structure, function, and homeostasis in the human body. Topics include the skeletal, muscular, integumentary, and nervous systems, as well as cytology, histology, and articulations. You'll use microscopic techniques, learn anatomical terminology, and apply physiological concepts to real clinical scenarios.

What You'll Learn

🦴
Skeletal & Muscular Systems
Bones, joints, muscles, calcium homeostasis, contraction physiology
🧠
Nervous System
Action potentials, CNS, PNS, special senses, autonomic division
🔬
Cells & Tissues
Histology, cell cycle, epithelial, connective, muscle & nervous tissue
⚖️
Homeostasis & Chemistry
Acid-base balance, fluid & electrolyte balance, pH, metabolism

Course Policies

The rules that keep things fair for everyone.

🚫 Category 1: No Second Chances (Zero Tolerance)

Labs, in-class activities, quizzes, and "windowed" assignments (open 5+ days) must be completed at the scheduled time or by the deadline. No make-ups, no late submissions. A missed item = zero.

🕐 Category 2: 5-School-Day Safety Net

Exams and standard assignments have a grace period of 5 school days, but a 25% flat deduction applies. After 5 days, the grade closes as a zero. For unexpected missed exams, you must email or text Dr. McNaughton the same day.

📵 Final Exam — No Exceptions

Final exams (common assessments) cannot be rescheduled or made up under any circumstances, except with official Accommodation Services documentation.

🤖 AI Usage Policy — Limited & Responsible

Permitted: Brainstorming, explaining concepts, grammar corrections, creating outlines, generating first drafts (must be significantly transformed into your own voice), image generation for creative projects.

Not Permitted: Copy-paste AI text as your own work, AI during exams or quizzes, fabricating citations or lab data, substituting AI analysis for your own evaluation.

Attribution required: Cite any AI tool used. If you can't explain your work in a follow-up, Path A (48-hr alternative assessment) is triggered.

🔬 Lab Policies

  • Closed-toe shoes required; hair must be secured; no food or drink.
  • Lab attendance is required — labs cannot be made up.
  • Follow all PPE rules and safety briefings at all times.
  • Failure to follow safety instructions may result in removal and loss of credit.

✉️ Communication Policy

Email is the primary contact method. Use your WITCC student email (first.last@my.witcc.edu), include the course number and your full name. Response time: within 48 business hours (Mon–Fri). For urgent matters, use subject line: "URGENT: [Topic]".

🎓 Academic Integrity

Path A (Suspicion): You'll be asked to complete a 48-hour alternative assessment to prove mastery. If you prove it, you keep your grade. If not — zero.

Path B (Confirmed): Immediate zero, no redo. A second offense = automatic F for the semester and possible dismissal.
