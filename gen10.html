Chapter 13: Translation & Proteins

Explore the mechanics of protein synthesis. Use the sections below to guide your study.

Translation requires three main components to polymerize amino acids:

  • Ribosomes: The "factory." 70S (prokaryotic) vs 80S (eukaryotic).
  • tRNA: The "adapter" that brings specific amino acids.
  • Aminoacyl tRNA Synthetase: The enzyme that "charges" the tRNA with the correct amino acid.

Translation occurs in three highly regulated phases:

  1. Initiation: Small subunit binds mRNA; initiator tRNA pairs with start codon.
  2. Elongation: Peptide bond formation occurs; the ribosome moves along the mRNA.
  3. Termination: Release factors bind to the stop codon; the complex dissociates.

Proteins serve diverse roles:

  • Enzymes: Catalyze reactions.
  • Immunoglobulins: Immune response.
  • Histones: DNA binding/compaction.
  • Transcription Factors: Gene expression regulation.
