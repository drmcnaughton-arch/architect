Ch. 13: Translation

Lecture Companion
Ribosomal Subunits

Ribosomes consist of large and small subunits[cite: 1639].

Bacterial (70S): 50S + 30S subunits[cite: 1791].
Eukaryotic (80S): 60S + 40S subunits[cite: 1792].
tRNA Charging

Aminoacyl tRNA synthetase chemically links tRNAs to their proper amino acids[cite: 1651].

There are 20 different synthetases—one for each amino acid[cite: 1652].
The Three Phases of Translation
1. Initiation: Requires IF1, IF2, IF3, and GTP [cite: 1656-1657].
2. Elongation: EF-Tu and EF-G facilitate the addition of amino acids [cite: 1657-1658].
3. Termination: Triggered by stop codons (UAG, UAA, UGA) and release factors [cite: 1677-1678].
Protein Structure Levels
  • Primary: Amino acid sequence[cite: 1730].
  • Secondary: Alpha-helices and Beta-sheets[cite: 1730].
  • Tertiary: 3D conformation[cite: 1730].
  • Quaternary: Multiple polypeptide chains[cite: 1731].
Histones
Proteins that bind to DNA in eukaryotes[cite: 1744].
