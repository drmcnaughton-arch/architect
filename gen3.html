Topic Summary Table

Topic Core idea Key study focus
Mendel’s Experimental Foundations Mendel used controlled pea plant crosses and quantitative records to identify patterns of inheritance. Traits, phenotypes, genotypes, alleles, homozygous, heterozygous.
Monohybrid Crosses A single trait can show predictable inheritance patterns across generations. 3:1 ratio, segregation, dominance, Punnett squares, testcrosses.
Dihybrid and Trihybrid Crosses Multiple traits can be analyzed using independent assortment and probability. 9:3:3:1 ratio, product law, forked-line method.
Probability and Chi-Square Probability explains expected ratios; chi-square evaluates whether observed data fit expectations. Null hypothesis, p value, degrees of freedom, goodness of fit.
Pedigrees Family trees reveal patterns of human inheritance. Pedigree symbols, autosomal dominant, autosomal recessive.
Tay-Sachs Disease A recessive disorder can be understood at the molecular level. Hex-A, ganglioside GM2, recessive inheritance, nervous system deterioration.
